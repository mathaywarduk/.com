The Wildcat Experience

Might be more fun after a few pints…

Yes that’s right… painted stone cats! Different painted wild cats are dotted about Newtonmore. From the post office in the village, you can get a book of the ones to find and a sheet/map to tick them off.

Loch an Eilein

30 mins from Newtonmore

A beautiful loch with a 13th Century island castle. walk around the loch in an hour, or hike up to the viewpoint and take in all the scenery.

More info

The Potting Shed

20 mins from Newtonmore

Eat cake and drink tea in beautiful surroundings of a garden nursery. While you’re there see Red Squirrels and all sorts of tits out the café window.

More info

Aviemore

30 mins from Newtonmore

A popular ski resort with more shops, bars and restaurants. If you head this way, you must visit our favourite pub/restaurant The Old Bridge Inn. The other one we’ve tried is The Winking Owl which also has great food, big dishes! Best to book ahead at both if you can.

Cairngorms National Park

Newtonmore is surrounded by The UK’s largest National Park which has everything from castles and distilleries to family attractions and endless outdoor fun, including watersports, snowsports, wildlife watching, and some incredible high and low level walking and cycling routes.

Dalwhinnie Distillery

20 mins from Newtonmore

Dalwhinnie Distillery is just 10 miles from Newtonmore where some of the world’s most famous malts are made.

Feeling adventurous? Loch Insh has sail sports, canoeing, kayaking, fishing, mountain biking, rowing, archery, play parks, and even a dry ski slope.
