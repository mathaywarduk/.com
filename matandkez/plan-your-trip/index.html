Getting there

Ok, we’re getting married in the middle of nowhere, so sorry about that. We’re going to drive up, so we can enjoy the lovely views and take all the important things with us.
(booze and sparklers)

Newtonmore is the closest town to the venue and there are lots of ways to get there. When you’re looking into planning your trip, the website Rome2Rio could come in handy. It’ll tell you the different ways you can get to Newtonmore and an idea of the cost. Any questions, just give us a shout.

Drive

Pack the car with your wedding outfit, wellies, a capri-sun and a enjoy a road trip up there. A car will also give you the freedom to travel around the beautiful Cairngorms area and maybe head on somewhere else after the wedding.
Don’t follow us though!

It takes roughly…

  • 6.5 hours from Nottingham/Derby to Newtonmore
  • 5 hours from Chorley to Newtonmore
  • 8.5 hours from London to Newtonmore
Get directions

Fly

Flights from the UK are only an hour or so, but by the time you’ve arrived at the airport for check-in and got your transport to Newtonmore at the other end, it’s probably a 3 - 5 hour journey.

From all airports you can hire a car, or catch a train or bus to the local area of Newtonmore.

The closest airports are…

  • Inverness: 1 hour away
  • Edinburgh: 2 hours away
  • Glasgow: 2.5 hours away

Price

£50 - £100 return flight, per person

Train or Bus

The train and buses will take you directly to Newtonmore, where most will probably be staying as it’s only 10 minutes from the wedding venue.

The train is approximately an 8hr journey from Nottingham/Derby and London.
Just enough time to have a few bottles of wine, play 20 rounds of rummy, and have a power nap.

Find train tickets

From London, Preston or Crewe you can also get the Caledonian Sleeper train, it takes a little bit longer but you travel through the night, arriving early morning with a full day still to go.

The bus is an alternative option if you’re looking for something cheap. National Express stops in Inverness, while Megabus go to Aviemore.
Buy a lot of puzzle books as it can be a 14hr journey!

Prices

Train: £150 - £180 return per person
Caledonian Sleeper train: £110 - £240 return per person
Bus Price: £80 return per person
